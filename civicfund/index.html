How CivicFund works

From idea to completed project — transparent at every step.

1

Post a project

An organizer describes the project, sets a funding goal, defines a budget, and sets a deadline.

2

Community pledges

Neighbors pledge funds. Money is authorized but not captured until the goal is reached — no goal, full refund.

3

Worker hired

Workers apply, the organizer selects one, and funds are captured into escrow. Work begins.

4

Community votes

Contributors vote on completion. Approved? Worker is paid via Stripe. Disputed? A fair review process kicks in.

Our promise

Your money only moves when the community says so

We built CivicFund on a simple principle: contributors should never lose their money to an incomplete project, and workers should never lose their pay to bad-faith rejection. Stripe holds all funds in escrow — we never touch them.

Goal not reached? Full automatic refund.

Funds are authorized, never captured, until the goal is met.

Community votes approve every payout.

Workers get paid when contributors confirm the work is done.

Every dollar is publicly accounted for.

Full disbursement ledger — see exactly how every cent was spent.

Workers are protected from unfair rejection.

Disputes go through a structured review — workers can't be stiffed.

Fund lifecycle

1

Pledge authorized

Card authorized, not charged. Cancels automatically if goal is missed.

2

Goal reached → captured into escrow

All pledges captured simultaneously when goal is met. Work begins.

3

Work in progress

Resource costs disbursed as receipts are submitted. Labor held until completion.

4

Community vote → worker paid

Contributors approve, worker receives full labor pay via Stripe Connect.
