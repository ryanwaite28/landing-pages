About CivicFund

We believe every neighborhood has the resources to improve itself — it just needs a trusted way to pool them.

Our mission

CivicFund exists to democratize community improvement. We give everyday people a trusted, transparent, and simple way to pool resources and pay for local change — without bureaucracy, grants, or government dependency.

The tools that large organizations use to fund and manage projects — escrow, contracts, payment verification — have never been accessible to ordinary communities. CivicFund changes that.

The problem we're solving

For contributors

Money raised for local projects disappears into PayPal links and Venmo requests with no accountability and no recourse if the project fails.

For organizers

Managing community projects means juggling spreadsheets, cash, Venmo, and group chats — with no real tools for transparency or accountability.

For workers

Local workers doing community jobs often don't get paid fairly — or at all — because there's no neutral party holding funds and enforcing completion.

Existing platforms like GoFundMe and Kickstarter solve fundraising but not project delivery. They have no mechanism for hiring workers, verifying completion, or disbursing funds in a transparent and accountable way. CivicFund fills that gap.

How we're different

Escrow-protected by design

Funds are held by Stripe and released only when the community votes work is complete. Contributors can't be defrauded; workers can't be stiffed.

Full financial transparency

Every project has a public disbursement ledger. Contributors see exactly how every dollar was spent — receipts, amounts, and dates — before any payout is approved.

Community governs completion

Contributors who funded a project vote on whether work meets the agreed standard. No single person decides — the community does.

Automatic refunds if goals aren't met

Pledges are authorized but not charged until a project hits its funding goal. Miss the goal or deadline? Every contributor is refunded automatically — no request needed.
